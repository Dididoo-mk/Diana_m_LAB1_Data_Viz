A2: Exploratory Visual Analysis: Corporate Landlords & Evictions

This project integrates Boston property and census data with court eviction filings to visually explore how corporate ownership correlates with housing instability across neighborhoods. By examining spatial and demographic trends, it reveals nuanced patterns in local eviction dynamics driven by institutional landlord practices.

A3: Visualization Critique & Redesign

For this assignment, we were asked to analyze and critique a well-known data visualization, looking at what works, what doesn’t, and how it could be improved. The visualization we examined comes from The New York Times, where it was used to illustrate the rise and fall of COVID-19 cases during the Omicron variant peak. The article was written by Dr. Jeffrey Shaman, an epidemiologist and infectious disease modeler at Columbia University, whose team helped develop some of the earliest COVID-19 models.

A4: Persuasive or Deceptive Visualization?

Data visualization is often perceived as a neutral medium for conveying information. However, visual design choices can significantly shape how data is interpreted, blurring the line between persuasion and deception. This assignment explores how intentional design decisions — from axis manipulation to selective data filtering — can influence a viewer’s understanding of a dataset.
